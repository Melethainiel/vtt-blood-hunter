# Blood Hunter Orders

At 3rd level, blood hunters commit to an order that grants unique abilities and defines their approach to hunting monsters.

## Order Selection

**Source:** [DND Beyond - Blood Hunter Orders](https://www.dndbeyond.com/classes/blood-hunter#BloodHunterOrder-1610847)

Choose one of the following orders:
- Order of the Ghostslayer
- Order of the Lycan
- Order of the Mutant
- Order of the Profane Soul

## Order Feature Progression

All orders grant features at the following levels:
- **3rd level:** Initial order features
- **7th level:** Order improvement
- **11th level:** Order improvement
- **15th level:** Order improvement
- **18th level:** Capstone order feature

## Available Orders

### Order of the Ghostslayer

**Focus:** Hunting undead and supernatural entities

The Order of the Ghostslayer is the oldest of the blood hunter orders, having originally existed as the very first blood hunters. These warriors specialize in the destruction of the undead and those who would pervert the natural order.

**Key Abilities:**
- Enhanced ability to track and destroy undead
- Supernatural awareness
- Protection against possession and curses
- Spectral abilities

**Playstyle:** Anti-undead specialist with defensive capabilities

**Full Details:** [Order of the Ghostslayer](http://dnd5e.wikidot.com/blood-hunter:ghostslayer)

---

### Order of the Lycan

**Focus:** Controlled lycanthropy and savage melee combat

The Order of the Lycan is a proud order of blood hunters who undergo "the Taming," a ceremonial inflicting of lycanthropy from a senior member. These blood hunters learn to control and harness their cursed affliction.

**Key Abilities:**
- Hybrid transformation (controlled lycanthrope form)
- Enhanced physical capabilities
- Natural weapons (claws, fangs)
- Predatory senses

**Playstyle:** Melee-focused shapeshifter with high damage output

**Full Details:** [Order of the Lycan](http://dnd5e.wikidot.com/blood-hunter:lycan)

---

### Order of the Mutant

**Focus:** Alchemical mutagens and adaptable combat

The Order of the Mutant is a collection of blood hunters who have dedicated their study to brewing toxic elixirs and alchemical concoctions. These mutagens grant temporary benefits but at physical and mental costs.

**Key Abilities:**
- Craft and consume mutagens
- Temporary ability enhancements
- Adaptable combat bonuses
- Alchemical mastery

**Playstyle:** Versatile combatant with customizable abilities

**Full Details:** [Order of the Mutant](http://dnd5e.wikidot.com/blood-hunter:mutant)

---

### Order of the Profane Soul

**Focus:** Warlock-style pact magic combined with blood magic

The Order of the Profane Soul represents blood hunters who have forged pacts with powerful entities, combining traditional hemocraft with eldritch magic. This dangerous path risks corruption but offers immense power.

**Key Abilities:**
- Pact magic (warlock spellcasting)
- Otherworldly patron
- Eldritch invocations
- Rite Focus (spell attacks can use crimson rite)

**Playstyle:** Hybrid caster/martial with magical versatility

**Special Multiclassing:** If you multiclass with warlock, add 1/3 of your blood hunter levels (rounded down) to your warlock level for spell slot progression. Consider aligning your patron between both classes.

**Full Details:** [Order of the Profane Soul](http://dnd5e.wikidot.com/blood-hunter:profane-soul)

## Choosing Your Order

### Combat Style Considerations

**Melee-Focused Builds**
- **Order of the Lycan:** Best for pure melee damage
- **Order of the Ghostslayer:** Good for balanced melee with defensive options

**Versatile/Utility Builds**
- **Order of the Mutant:** Highly adaptable to different situations
- **Order of the Profane Soul:** Magic + martial versatility

**Ranged/Casting Builds**
- **Order of the Profane Soul:** Only order with spellcasting

### Campaign Considerations

**Undead-Heavy Campaigns**
- Order of the Ghostslayer is ideal

**Varied Enemy Types**
- Order of the Mutant offers the most flexibility

**Roleplay-Heavy Campaigns**
- Order of the Lycan and Profane Soul offer interesting character dynamics and internal struggles

### Party Composition

Consider what your party needs:
- **Lack of magic?** → Order of the Profane Soul
- **Need more damage?** → Order of the Lycan
- **Need versatility?** → Order of the Mutant
- **Fighting undead?** → Order of the Ghostslayer
